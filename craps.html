Rules of Craps

  • You must place a bet before the starting roll (when indicator is 'Off')
  • A 'Pass' bet is done at the beginning. If the roller lands a 7 or 11, you win. If they roll a 2, 3, or 12, you lose.
  • If the above condition isn't met, the value of the dice becomes the 'point', marked by the indicator being 'On'. With a pass bet, the roller must land the point again before a 7 is rolled in order to win.
  • A 'Don't Pass' bet is very similar, but opposite. The win conditions are reversed compared to a 'Pass' bet (7 or 11 first roll is a loss; 2 or 3 is a win; 12 is a loss due to this table being 'Don't Pass Bar Double Six'; if it goes to 'point' rolls, a 7 wins and the point loses)
  • A 'Field' bet is done by placing chips on the number you bet will be rolled next. You win if it is rolled, and lose if it isn't.
  • A 'One Roll' bet bets that the next roll will be the number you bet on. Otherwise, its a loss.
  • A 'Hardway' bet bets that the roller will get the selected double before rolling a 7 or the value of the dice without the double (e.g. bet is 3,3 and 4,2 is rolled. Result = loss)
  • Pass / Don't Pass : 1:1
  • Field:
    • 3,4,9,10,11 : 1:1
    • 2: 2:1
    • 12: 3:1
  • One Roll:
    • Any Craps (2,3,12): 7:1
    • 2 or 12: 30:1
    • 3 or 11: 15:1
  • Hardway:
    • 4 or 10: 7:1
    • 6 or 8: 9:1
